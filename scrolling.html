Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) is an Indian multinational technology company specializing in information technology services and consulting. Headquartered in Mumbai, it is a part of the Tata Group and operates in 150 locations across 46 countries.[4] It is the second-largest Indian company by market capitalization.

About

Tata Consultancy Services Limited, originally known as Tata Computer Systems, was established in 1968 by Tata Sons Limited.[12] The company's initial contracts involved providing punched card services to its sister company TISCO (now Tata Steel), developing an Inter-Branch Reconciliation System for the Central Bank of India,[13] and offering bureau services to the Unit Trust of India

Operations

As of September 2023, TCS had a total of 51 subsidiary companies in 55 countries.[82][83] As of 2024, Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) operates from over 50 countries and more than 500 offices globally.[84] In India, TCS has multiple delivery centers in major cities, including Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai, and Hyderabad.[85] It has significant operations in key markets such as the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, and Singapore.[86][87]

Employees

Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) is one of the largest private-sector employers in India,[106] and ranks as the fourth-largest employer overall, following Indian Railways, Indian Army, and India Post.[107] As of 8 July 2022, TCS had over 600,000 employees.[108][109][110][111][112] As of 31 March 2013, the number of non-Indian nationals employed by TCS was 21,282, representing 7.7% of the workforce.[108][113] In 2008, TCS was the fifth-largest recipient of H-1B visas in the United States, following Infosys, Cognizant, Wipro, and Satyam.[114][115][116] In 2012, TCS and other Tata Group companies were the second-largest recipients of H-1B visas.[117][9] Subramaniam Ramadorai, the former CEO of TCS, authored an autobiographical book titled The TCS Story...and Beyond about his experiences with the company.[118] List of CEOs F. Sponsorships

TCS is the title sponsor for Toronto Waterfront Marathon, London Marathon, Amsterdam Marathon, Mumbai Marathon, Lidingöloppet, and New York City Marathon[120][121] and one of the sponsors of City2Surf, Australian Running Festival, Berlin Marathon, Chicago Marathon, and Boston Marathon.[122] In India, it is the title sponsor of World 10K held in Bangalore every year.[123] TCS has been a sponsor of the Indian Premier League team Rajasthan Royals since 2009.[124] It also provides the team with technology to help in the analysis of player performance, simulation, and use of RFID tags for tracking the players’ fitness levels and for security purposes in the stadiums.[125][126] TCS conducts an annual IT quiz for high school students called TCS IT Wiz.[127] TCS also currently sponsors Jaguar Racing in Formula E and Nakajima Racing in Super Formula

Controversies

On 14 February 2006, the US law firm Lieff Cabraser filed a nationwide class action lawsuit against TCS concerning payments to employees on deputation.[129] This lawsuit was filed on behalf of all non-US citizens employed by TCS in California from 14 February 2002 to 30 June 2005. The workers alleged that they were compelled to forfeit their federal and state tax refunds to their employer and that their Indian salaries were wrongfully deducted from their US pay.[130] On 22 February 2013, TCS agreed to settle the class-action suit for $30 million.[131] In July 2013, Judge Claudia Wilken of the US District Court, Northern District of California in Oakland, California, granted final approval for the settlement of the lawsuit. Trade secret lawsuit In a lawsuit filed in October 2014 in the US District Court in Madison, Epic Systems accused TCS and Tata America International Corp of "brazenly stealing the trade secrets, confidential information, documents, and data" belonging to Epic. This lawsuit was amended in January and December 2015. In April 2016, a US jury imposed a US$940 million fine on TCS and Tata America International Corp in the trade secret case,[132] which was reduced to US$420 million in 2017.[133] In 2020, an appeals court ruled that the US$280 million in punitive damages was "constitutionally excessive", but upheld the remaining portion of US$140 million in compensatory damages.[134] The case concluded in November 2023, with TCS agreeing to pay US$125 million
